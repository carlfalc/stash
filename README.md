# Stash

Stash is a gamified household inventory management app that turns the tedious task of tracking possessions into an engaging collecting experience. Users scan items to build their digital 'stash', earning points, achievements, and valuable insights about their consumption patterns. With rising inflation and minimalism trends, people need better ways to know what they own, avoid duplicate purchases, and make informed decisions about their belongings.

## About

People waste hundreds of dollars annually buying items they already own but can't find or forgot about. The average household has $3,000+ in unused items buried in closets, garages, and storage. When moving, organizing, or shopping, people struggle to remember what they own, leading to duplicate purchases of tools, cables, medications, pantry items, and household goods. Insurance claims are difficult without proper inventory records. Current solutions are boring spreadsheets or expensive enterprise systems - nothing makes personal inventory management enjoyable or rewarding.

## Market Opportunity

The global household inventory market is valued at $1.2B and growing 12% annually, driven by insurance requirements and organizational trends. In NZ/AU, 2.8M households actively use organization apps, with average spending of $180/year on inventory-related tools and storage solutions. The broader 'life organization' app market (including Todoist, Notion) generates $2.4B annually. Target market: 15M English-speaking households globally who actively manage their possessions, with initial focus on 800K NZ/AU households earning $60K+ annually.

## Tech Stack

I scored 8 because: (1) Core functionality uses well-established mobile APIs - camera access, local storage, cloud sync are solved problems, (2) Image recognition can leverage existing services like Google Vision API rather than building custom ML, (3) Gamification features are standard app development patterns. Minor concerns around image storage costs at scale and ensuring offline functionality, but nothing that blocks an MVP launch.

## Point of Difference

Stash is the first gamified inventory system - users earn 'Collector' badges, streak rewards, and can compete with friends on organization challenges. Our AI categorizes items intelligently and suggests related items you might own. Integration with shopping apps prevents duplicate purchases by alerting when you already own similar items. The 'Stash Score' gamifies decluttering and organization, making boring inventory management genuinely fun.

## Target Users

Primary: Organized millennials (25-40) who love apps, games, and optimization. Often homeowners with disposal income, appreciate technology solutions. Secondary: Families preparing for insurance claims or moves. Tertiary: Minimalist-curious consumers wanting to audit their possessions. All segments value time-saving and enjoy achievement-based apps.
